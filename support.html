Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Success Rate number mean?

It's the percentage of simulated retirement scenarios in which your portfolio lasts your full planning horizon. A 95% success rate means 95 out of 100 simulated futures ended with money remaining. It is not a guarantee — it's a probability based on historical-style return and inflation assumptions.

Is my financial data stored in the cloud?

No. All data is stored locally on your device and encrypted. SuccessRate does not connect to any server, cloud service, or external database. Your data never leaves your device.

How do I back up my plans?

Your plans are saved in a local database file on your device. You can back it up manually by copying the database file to another location (e.g., an external drive or cloud folder of your choice). SuccessRate does not perform automatic backups.

What is Monte Carlo simulation?

Monte Carlo simulation runs hundreds of randomized retirement scenarios using different sequences of market returns and inflation. Rather than assuming a single average return, it shows you the range of possible outcomes — helping you understand best-case, worst-case, and median results.

What Social Security number should I enter?

Enter your estimated annual Social Security benefit in today's dollars. You can find your personalized estimate at ssa.gov/myaccount. If you're unsure, a rough estimate still produces useful results — you can always update it later.

How do I compare different retirement scenarios?

Use the Save button to save your current plan, then adjust your inputs (e.g., spending, claiming age, allocation) and save as a new plan. You can create as many plans as you like and switch between them to compare outcomes.

Is SuccessRate a substitute for a financial advisor?

No. SuccessRate is a planning tool that provides probabilistic projections to help you think through retirement scenarios. It is not personalized financial advice. For decisions involving significant assets, consult a qualified financial professional.
