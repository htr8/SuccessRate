Why the Math Matters

Most Calculators Cut Corners. This One Doesn't.

Honesty over false precision — including the bad outcomes most tools hide from you.

Fat Tails

Crashes Happen More Than the Bell Curve Predicts

Standard models underestimate tail risk. SuccessRate uses a Student-t distribution (df=5), so rare catastrophic market events occur with realistic frequency — not wishful statistics.

Regime Switching

A Major Crash Early Retirement Is Far More Damaging Than at Year 20

Two separate market regimes — normal and stress — with Markov transitions model the real clustering of downturns. Sequence-of-returns risk is modeled, not glossed over.

Tax Precision

Progressive federal and state brackets, IRMAA Medicare surcharges, RMDs, and capital gains stacking — calculated year by year. Not estimated. Not averaged. Calculated.

Action Plan

Five Levers That Move Your Success Rate

Run baseline → test one change at a time → combine improvements → re-run annually.

1
Reduce Spending Rate
Single most powerful lever. Dropping from 5% to 4% improves success more than almost any investment change.
2
Diversify Globally
At high US valuations, international stocks carry higher expected returns. Test 40/20/10/30 US/Intl/EM/Bonds.
3
Dynamic Spending
Guardrails strategy typically adds 4–8 percentage points to success rate vs. rigid fixed withdrawal.
4
2-Year Cash Buffer
Draw from cash during downturns. Improves the 5th percentile floor significantly.
5
Reduce Tax Drag
Roth conversions before RMDs + optimal withdrawal ordering. Can add 5–10 percentage points to after-tax wealth.
